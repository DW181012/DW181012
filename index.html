Welcome!

I am a 1st-year PhD student in Applied Math at Imperial College London, supervised by Dr Eric Keaveny. My research interests are mainly in active matter and biological fluid dynamics. Currently, we are studying the coordination of ciliary beating. We are also teaming up with Dr. Janna Nawroth's group at Helmholtz Pioneer Campus to study its effect on mucociliary clearance (MCC), a biophysical mechanism clearing the inhaled pollutant particles.

Previously, I earned my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Mathematics at New York University, where I joined the Applied Math Lab and worked with Prof. Leif Ristroph and Dr. Christiana Mavroyiakoumou on collective bird flights.
